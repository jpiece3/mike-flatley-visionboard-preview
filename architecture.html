3. Selection Flow

| Step | Dimension | Selection Type | Heading | Subheading | Min | Max |

|------|-----------|----------------|---------|------------|-----|-----|

| 1 | style | cardselection | Choose Your Home Style | What vibe speaks to you? | 1 | 1 |

| 2 | neighborhoodvibe | cardselection | Pick Your Neighborhood | Where do you see yourself? | 1 | 1 |

| 3 | bedrooms | scaleselector | How Much Space? | Slide to your ideal size | 1 | 1 |

| 4 | musthaves | chipmultiselect | Pick Your Must-Haves | Choose up to 5 features you can't live without | 1 | 5 |

| 5 | budget | scaleselector | Your Investment Range | No judgment -- just matching | 1 | 1 |

| 6 | timeline | cardselection | When Are You Moving? | We'll match your urgency | 1 | 1 |

Flow design rationale:
  • Steps 1-2 (cardselection, cardselection): Visual, engaging, identity-forming. Step 1 uses large image cards, Step 2 uses medium icon cards -- visually distinct. The user starts by dreaming.
  • Step 3 (scaleselector): Quick, low-friction tap on a segmented bar. Breaks the card pattern. Practical but still about their life (family size, not finances).
  • Step 4 (chipmultiselect): The most expressive step. Multi-select chips let users build their wishlist. This is where micro-commitment peaks.
  • Steps 5-6 (scaleselector, cardselection): Practical, decision-oriented. Budget as a quiet scale, timeline as small cards. These are qualification dimensions but framed casually.
Selection type variety: 3 different types used (cardselection, scaleselector, chipmultiselect). No two consecutive steps use identical visual presentations.

Fallback Profile

Profile: The Nester Rationale: The Nester represents the largest buyer segment in Chester County (growing families, 40-50% of traffic per research). When selections are too diffuse to clearly match a specific profile, they most likely fit the broad family-oriented buyer that Chester County primarily attracts.

Match Algorithm

For each profile:

  usertags = all tags from user's selections across all 6 dimensions


  overlap = count(usertags INTERSECT profile.triggertags)


  ratio = overlap / count(profile.triggertags)


  if ratio >= profile.matchthreshold (0.5): candidate



Winner = candidate with highest ratio


Ties broken by profile order (first in array wins)


If no candidate exceeds threshold: fallback to The Nester

5. Qualification Signals (Backend Only)

These signals are NEVER shown to the user. They determine email sequence targeting and follow-up priority.

Urgency (from Timeline Dimension)

| Level | Tags | Meaning |

|-------|------|---------|

| Hot | immediate, urgent, active-search | Ready to tour homes this week. Same-day follow-up. |

| Warm | 3-months, 6-months, planning, early-planning | Actively researching. Nurture with neighborhood guides. |

| Cool | browsing, exploring, next-year | Long-term dreamer. Monthly market updates. |

Budget Fit (from Budget Dimension)

| Level | Tags | Meaning |

|-------|------|---------|

| Hot | budget-premium, budget-luxury | $750K+ -- above team's average sale price. High-value lead. |

| Warm | budget-mid, budget-upper | $300-750K -- core Chester County market. Solid lead. |

| Cool | budget-starter | Under $300K -- limited Chester County inventory. Longer nurture. |

Composite Formula

composite = urgency  0.6 + budgetfit  0.4
Signal values: hot = 1.0, warm = 0.5, cool = 0.0 Composite thresholds:
  • >= 0.7 = Hot lead (immediate outreach, schedule showing)
  • 0.3 - 0.69 = Warm lead (nurture sequence, market updates)
  • < 0.3 = Cool lead (long-term drip, inspiration content)
Weight rationale: Urgency weighted higher (0.6) because real estate is timing-driven. The Gary Mercer Team's volume model (401 sales/year) means they can serve all budget levels -- urgency is the stronger signal.
