Consultation Scripts - Vision Board Follow-Up

Mike Flatley | The Gary Mercer Team
Conversation frameworks for hot/warm/cool leads from vision board

Script Philosophy

These are frameworks, not verbatim scripts. Use your own voice. The goal: diagnose needs, provide value, build trust. Never sound like you're reading. Key Principles:
  1. Listen more than you talk (70/30 rule)
  2. Ask questions before offering solutions
  3. Reference their wishlist (shows you paid attention)
  4. No pressure, just guidance
  5. End every call with a clear next step

Hot Lead Script (ASAP / Within 3 Months Timeline)

Context

  • They selected "ASAP" or "Within 3 Months" on timeline
  • Budget $500K+ (serious buyer)
  • Completed wishlist within last 7 days

Opening (First 60 seconds)

You: Hi [First Name], this is Sharra from The Gary Mercer Team. You built a Dream Home Wishlist a few days ago and said you're looking to move soon. Do you have 10 minutes to chat? [Wait for confirmation] You: Great! I pulled up your wishlist — you're [Profile Name], right? [Style], [Neighborhood vibe], [Must-haves]. Love it.

Before we dive into listings, can I ask: what's driving your timeline? Are you relocating for work, lease ending, family situation?

[Listen. This is the most important question.]

Diagnosis Phase (Minutes 2-5)

Key Questions to Ask:
  1. Pre-approval status:

"Have you been pre-approved yet, or is that your next step?"

  • If yes → "Who's your lender? Are you happy with them?"
  • If no → "I've got 3 lenders who move fast. Want an intro?"
  1. Other agents:

"Are you working with another agent, or just starting your search?"

  • If yes → "How's that going? What do you wish was different?"
  • If no → "Good. Let me tell you how we work."
  1. Deal-breakers:

"I saw your must-haves on the wishlist. Is there anything that's an absolute no? Like, you'd walk away from a perfect house if it had X?"

  • Listen for: school district, commute time, HOA, condition
  1. Budget reality check:

"You selected [$XXX-$XXX] on your wishlist. Is that where you're comfortable, or were you just exploring?"

  • If exploring → Adjust expectations now
  • If locked in → Confirm what they can get in that range

Consultation Phase (Minutes 6-8)

You: Okay, here's what I'm thinking. Based on your [Profile] wishlist and your timeline, we should tour 5-7 homes this week or next.

I've already pulled a list that matches your [must-haves] in [neighborhoods]. Some just hit the market. A couple won't last the week — inventory in your range is tight right now.

[Show them you've done the work already] You: Before we schedule, let me set expectations: The market right now:
  • Inventory is up 20% from last year, so you've got choices
  • Rates are around 6%, which is stable
  • Good homes in the [$XXX-$XXX] range still move fast, but you have negotiating power
  • We're seeing price reductions and closing cost credits again
How I work:
  • I'll send you 5-7 properties to review tonight
  • Pick your top 3, and I'll schedule showings
  • We tour together — I'll point out things other agents miss
  • When you find the one, I'll help you craft an offer that wins without overpaying
[Pause. Let them process.]

Closing Phase (Minutes 9-10)

You: Sound good? [Wait for yes] You: Perfect. What does your schedule look like this week? Morning, afternoon, or evening? [Lock in specific day/time] You: I'll send you the property list by [tonight/tomorrow morning] with my notes on each one. Reply with your top 3, and I'll confirm showing times.

One more thing: if anything pops up between now and then that matches your wishlist, I'll text you. Sound good?

[Get phone number if you only have email] You: Great. Talk soon, [First Name]. Let's find your home.

Follow-Up Actions (Within 24 Hours)

  1. Send custom property list (5-7 homes with agent notes)
  2. Text confirmation with your direct number
  3. Add to CRM with "Hot - Tours Scheduled" tag
  4. Set reminder to follow up if they don't reply in 24 hours

Warm Lead Script (3-12 Month Timeline)

Context

  • They selected "3-6 Months" or "Next Year" on timeline
  • May or may not have pre-approval yet
  • Researching neighborhoods and prices

Opening (First 60 seconds)

You: Hi [First Name], this is Sharra from The Gary Mercer Team. You built a Dream Home Wishlist last week — loved your [Profile Name] selections. Do you have a few minutes? [Wait for confirmation] You: I'm not calling to push you to buy tomorrow. I know you said you're [3-6 months / next year] out. I just wanted to introduce myself and see if you have any questions about the Chester County market. [Immediately reduce pressure]

Diagnosis Phase (Minutes 2-5)

Key Questions to Ask:
  1. Timeline driver:

"What's making you think [3-6 months / next year]? Is it saving for a down payment, waiting for a lease to end, just getting started?"

  • Listen for: financial barriers vs. life timing
  1. Familiarity with Chester County:

"Are you local, or are you relocating here?"

  • If relocating → Offer neighborhood guide
  • If local → "Which neighborhoods are you considering?"
  1. Biggest uncertainty:

"What's the biggest question mark right now? Budget? Neighborhoods? When to start looking?"

  • This tells you what value to provide
  1. Pre-approval:

"Have you talked to a lender yet, or is that on your to-do list?"

  • If not yet → "No rush, but I'd do that sooner than later. Want a referral?"

Consultation Phase (Minutes 6-8)

You: Okay, here's my take. You're [3-6 months / next year] out, which is actually perfect timing. Here's why: Why starting now is smart:
  1. You get to browse without pressure
  2. You'll learn what you actually want vs. what you think you want
  3. When the right one pops up, you're ready to move
What I recommend:
  1. Get pre-approved in the next 30 days (even if you're not ready to buy yet)
  2. Start casually touring homes — open houses, no appointment needed
  3. I'll send you listings that match your [Profile] wishlist once a week
  4. When you're 60-90 days out, we'll get serious
[Frame it as low-pressure education, not sales]

Closing Phase (Minutes 9-10)

You: Sound fair? [Wait for yes] You: Cool. I'm not going to bug you. Here's what happens next:
  1. I'll send you a Chester County neighborhood guide (matches your [neighborhood vibe] preference)
  2. You'll get weekly listing updates — no spam, just homes that fit your wishlist
  3. When you're 2-3 months out, reply to one of my emails and we'll schedule tours

If anything changes and you want to move faster, just call me. If your timeline pushes out, totally fine — just let me know.

[Give them permission to take their time] You: Questions before I let you go? [Answer, then close] You: Perfect. I'll send that neighborhood guide tonight. Enjoy the research phase — it's actually kind of fun when there's no pressure. Talk soon.

Follow-Up Actions (Within 24 Hours)

  1. Send neighborhood guide (PDF or link to market snapshot)
  2. Add to weekly nurture list (email sequence)
  3. Set 60-day follow-up reminder (check-in call)
  4. Tag in CRM as "Warm - Nurture Track"

Cool Lead Script (Just Looking / 12+ Months)

Context

  • They selected "Just Looking" or long timeline
  • May not be serious buyer yet
  • Could be dreaming, researching, or very early stage

Opening (First 60 seconds)

You: Hi [First Name], Sharra from The Gary Mercer Team. You built a Dream Home Wishlist — I'm calling everyone who completes it just to say hi. Do you have 2 minutes? [Wait for confirmation] You: I saw you picked "Just Looking" for your timeline, which is totally fine. I'm not calling to sell you a house today. I'm just curious: are you actively thinking about buying, or just browsing for fun? [Let them tell you where they are]

Diagnosis Phase (Minutes 2-4)

Key Questions to Ask:
  1. Motivation level:

"On a scale of 1-10, where 1 is 'I was just bored and took a quiz' and 10 is 'I'm ready to move tomorrow,' where are you?"

  • Honest answer tells you how to proceed
  1. Barriers:

"What would need to happen for you to actually buy? Like, what's holding you back?"

  • Listen for: money, timing, job, family, uncertainty
  1. Renting vs. owning:

"Are you renting now, or do you already own and thinking about moving?"

  • Renter → Education about affordability, programs
  • Owner → Timeline for selling current home

Consultation Phase (Minutes 5-6)

You: Got it. Here's what I'll do:

I'll add you to my monthly market update list. It's not salesy — just "here's what happened in Chester County this month" kind of stuff. You can see what homes are selling for, what inventory looks like, all that.

If you ever want to know "what could I get for $XXX,XXX in Chester County," just reply to one of those emails. I'll send you examples.

And if your timeline changes — you get a new job, you inherit money, you win the lottery — call me. I'll help you figure it out.

[No pressure. Just planting seeds.]

Closing Phase (Minutes 7-8)

You: Sound okay? [Wait for yes] You: Cool. Enjoy the browsing phase. When you're ready, I'm here.

Oh, one more thing — follow us on Instagram (@sharramercer). We post Chester County neighborhood stuff, market updates, behind-the-scenes. It's actually interesting, I promise.

[Soft social media CTA] You: That's it. Thanks for taking my call. Good luck with the search, whenever that actually starts.

Follow-Up Actions (Within 24 Hours)

  1. Add to monthly market update list (low-touch drip)
  2. Tag in CRM as "Cool - Long Nurture"
  3. Set 6-month follow-up reminder (check if anything changed)
  4. No immediate follow-up (respect their timeline)

Objection Handling Scripts

Objection 1: "I'm already working with another agent"

You: Got it. How's that going? Are they taking care of you? [Listen. Most people will vent if they're not happy.] If they say it's fine: You: Great. If it's not broke, don't fix it. But if anything changes, keep my number. I'm always here. If they hesitate or complain: You: Look, I'm not going to poach you. But if you're not getting the service you deserve — like, they're not responsive, they're not strategic, whatever — you're allowed to switch. Just saying.

Objection 2: "I need to talk to my spouse first"

You: Of course. Should I call back when they're available, or do you want to talk to them first and circle back? [Give them control] You: What's the main thing you need to align on? Budget? Timeline? Neighborhood? [This tells you what the real blocker is]

Objection 3: "I'm not sure I can afford Chester County"

You: What price range were you thinking? [Let them state their budget] You: Okay, here's the truth. Chester County median is around $629K, but we've got homes from $300K to $3M. It really depends on what you're willing to compromise on. [Pull up 2-3 examples in their budget] You: These are in your range. They might not have everything on your wishlist, but they're real options. Want me to send you a full list so you can see what's possible? [Education > pressure]

Objection 4: "I'm just not ready yet"

You: Totally fine. When do you think you will be? [Get specific timeline] You: Perfect. I'll check in with you [2 months before that date]. In the meantime, want me to send you listings so you can stay in the loop? [Low-commitment offer]

Script Add-Ons & Personalization

If They're a Specific Profile, Use Profile Language

The Minimalist:

"I saw you're a Minimalist — clean lines, smart home, quality over quantity. We've got some great modern homes in West Chester Borough that check those boxes."

The Nester:

"You're a Nester, right? Big yard, top schools, room to grow. Have you looked at the Great Valley or Unionville-Chadds Ford school districts yet?"

The Entertainer:

"Entertainer profile — love it. I've got a listing with a gourmet kitchen and outdoor kitchen that you need to see. Literally built for hosting."

The Urbanite:

"Urbanite = walkable vibes. Are you familiar with Phoenixville's Bridge Street? It's having a moment right now."

The Retreater:

"You're a Retreater — peace, privacy, sanctuary. Have you considered Unionville or Kennett Square? Horse country vibes, acreage, rolling hills."

Call Wrap-Up Checklist

Before you hang up, make sure you:

  • [ ] Confirmed next step (tour, email follow-up, callback date)
  • [ ] Got their preferred contact method (text, email, call)
  • [ ] Referenced their wishlist profile (shows you paid attention)
  • [ ] Set expectations (no pressure, strategic guidance)
  • [ ] Left them feeling heard, not sold

Follow-Up Cadence by Lead Type

Hot Leads

  • Day 0: Initial call
  • Day 1: Send property list
  • Day 2: Confirm tour times
  • Day 3-7: Tour homes
  • Day 8: Post-tour debrief
  • Weekly: Check-in until they find a home

Warm Leads

  • Day 0: Initial call
  • Day 1: Send neighborhood guide
  • Week 1: Weekly listing emails start
  • Month 2: Check-in call ("How's the search going?")
  • Month 3: Ramp up to tours

Cool Leads

  • Day 0: Initial call
  • Day 1: Add to monthly market updates
  • Month 3: "Still thinking about buying?" email
  • Month 6: Follow-up call
  • Month 12: Final check-in or long-term nurture

Call Recording & Training

Best Practice: Record calls (with permission) for training purposes. What to listen for:
  1. Are you talking more than listening? (Should be 30/70)
  2. Are you asking open-ended questions?
  3. Are you referencing their wishlist?
  4. Are you creating next steps?
  5. Are you removing pressure or adding it?
Improve: Listen to your own calls monthly. Refine based on what works.
